Infiniteness and Linear Temporal Logic

Eric Campbell, advised by Michael Greenberg

We submitted the manuscript to Pomona College for May 2017. Presentation slides can be found here.

Abstract

We present sound and complete axiomatic framework for Linear Temporal Logic over finite traces (LTLf ), as well as a decision procedure. Our work is founded completeness proofs for LTL [Kroger and Merz. 2008], Past-time framings, [Lichtenstein et al. 1985], and anchored frameworks [Manna and Pnueli. 1988]. Further we rely on the an extant axiomatization of LTLf [Becket et al. 2016] and an extant semantics [DeGiacomo and Vardi. 2013,2015,2016]. Our approach follows the least fixpoint method for LTL completeness and decidability given in [Kroger and Merz 2016], using insights about finitude presented. Finally, the completeness of LTLf extends the partial completeness proof for Temporal Netkat to a full completeness result.
